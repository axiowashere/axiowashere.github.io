BACKGROUND

From 2000 to 2011, Seven Indigenous students were found dead in Thunder Bay Ontario.

They were hundreds of kilometers away, forced to leave to attend an adequate high school.

Out of the seven , five were found dead on the rivers surrounding Lake Superior.

With a sweeping narrative, investigative journalist Tanya Talega delves into the history of this city that has manifested Canada's long struggle with human rights violations and indigenous communities.

MORE ABOUT THE BOOK

"IN 1996, THE LAST RESIDENTIAL SCHOOL WAS FINALLY SHUT DOWN."

About 150,000 children had been sent to these schools, passing down the horror and trauma of the experience to their children, grandchildren, nieces and nephews.

In 1978, the Northern Nishnawbe Education Council was established, to run the Indigenous people's education under their own governance.

In September 2000, Pelican Falls High School opens their doors to up to 150 Indigenous students enrolling each year, boarding to this location up to 500 kilometers away from home.

Out of the 150 students, there were Seven Feathers here.
